Data Journalism Training materials

This is a collection of my data journalism training materials, primarily used for a college-level course I teach at the University of Minnesota. However, it could also be used by someone wishing to learn on their own. Many (although not all) of the exercises have directions that walk you through. If you have problems with any of the files or links here, please contact me via Twitter, @MaryJoWebster


Recommended books


  • "Computer-Assisted Reporting: A Practical Guide," 4th edition. Available at the U of M bookstore or for online purchase at IRE.org or on Amazon.com.
  • Data Journalism Handbook
  • Computer-Assisted Reporting, A comprehensive primer, by Fred Vallance-Jones and David McKie. Available from the IRE bookstore
  • "Numbers in the Newsroom," by Sarah Cohen. Available from the IRE Bookstore
  • "Precision Journalism: A Reporter's Introduction to Social Science Methods," 4th edition, by Philip Meyer. Available through online bookstores.
