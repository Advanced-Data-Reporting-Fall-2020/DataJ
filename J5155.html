Data Journalism Class

Spring 2017

This is the schedule and class materials for J-5155 at the University of Minnesota School of Journalism and Mass Communication.

Required materials:


  • Subscription ($40 for non-IRE members, $25 for IRE members) NICAR-LEARN video tutorial site
  • Syllabus
  • Moodle
  • Directions for story assignment

Software we'll be using (all free):


You will likely want to load this on your personal computer for homework assignments (either MacOS or Windows):

  • Spreadsheets: Either Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets
  • Database manager: SQL Lite add-on for Firefox
  • Geographic Information System software: QGIS (choose "latest release")
  • Data cleaning software: Google Refine 2.5 (earlier stable version of Open Refine)
  • Data visualization software: Tableau Public

We will also use some online tools that will require you to create a free account:

  • Online mapping: Carto
  • Online graphics: DataWrapper
  • Convert PDFs: Cometdocs

Recommended books


  • Computer-Assisted Reporting: A Practical Guide, 4th edition, by Brant Houston. (Also on Amazon.com)
  • Data Literacy, by David Herzog (also available through IRE)
  • The Curious Journalist's Guide to Data, by Jonathan Stray(e-book)
  • Data Journalism Handbook (e-book)
  • Computer-Assisted Reporting, A comprehensive primer, by Fred Vallance-Jones and David McKie
  • Numbers in the Newsroom, by Sarah Cohen (e-book)
  • Precision Journalism: A Reporter's Introduction to Social Science Methods , 4th edition, by Philip Meyer

Get inspired: examples of good data-driven stories


  • IRE's Extra! Extra! blog -a great place to find inspiration
  • 60 data-driven stories
